Chapter 01

Introduction

Parallelism is Monad's defining property. PEV is the developer-facing lens on it, a tool that answers, at a glance, whether a contract is killing that property, and why.

P. 002
Principles
01   Clarity before aesthetics.
The design defers to the data. Decoration exists only where it helps a developer see cause and effect faster.
02   Causality over summary.
A dashboard shows what is. PEV shows what caused what. The brand should feel investigative, not reportorial.
03   Technical, but literate.
Monospace for the truth, hashes, slots, gas. Serif for the voice. Sans for the interface. A three-voice system that stays distinct.

Chapter 02

The mark

Four stacked bars of unequal length, an abstraction of the execution timeline itself. Each bar is a thread; each length, a transaction's duration. The logo is the product, in miniature.

P. 008
pev.
Construction
Rationale

Four bars represent the four execution states the tool reveals: clean, delayed, re-executed, and conflict. Their unequal lengths mirror a real timeline, never symmetrical, always informative.

The wordmark pairs a serif with an ember period. The period is a halt, the stop-moment a developer wants to understand.

Clearspace & minimum size
Clearspace = height of one bar (3u) on all sides.
Minimum size: 16px (digital), 8mm (print).
